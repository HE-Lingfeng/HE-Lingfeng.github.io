Research Focus

From visual evidence to semantic reasoning.

I study how models perceive, localize, reason, and edit visual content under limited supervision or implicit instructions. My work connects representation learning, contrastive objectives, multimodal language models, and diffusion-based generation.

Reasoning-Guided Generative Image Editing

Co-first author

This project explores generative image editing driven by semantic reasoning rather than explicit low-level instructions. It introduces a reasoning attention mechanism to bridge multimodal language understanding and visual synthesis, enabling edits that depend on world knowledge, abstract intent, and implicit user needs.

Weakly-supervised ROI Extraction Method Based on Contrastive Learning for Remote Sensing Images

First author

This work studies weakly supervised region-of-interest extraction for remote sensing images. It uses Grad-CAM based pseudo labels and a contrastive learning objective to mitigate label noise by pulling positive samples closer while separating positive and negative pairs.
